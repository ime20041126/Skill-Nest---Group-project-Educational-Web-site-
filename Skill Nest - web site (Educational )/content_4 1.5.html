Lesson 5: Introduction to Algebra

Build your foundation in algebra by learning variables, expressions, and solving equations step-by-step.

What is Algebra?

Algebra is the branch of mathematics that uses symbols and letters (called variables) to represent numbers and quantities in equations and formulas. It is a powerful tool used to describe patterns, solve problems, and understand relationships between values.

  • Variable: A letter that represents an unknown number (e.g., x, y, z).
  • Constant: A fixed number (e.g., 5, -2, 100).
  • Expression: A combination of variables, constants, and operators (e.g., 2x + 5).
  • Equation: A mathematical statement with an “equals” sign (e.g., 2x + 5 = 15).

Understanding Algebraic Expressions

Algebraic expressions do not contain equal signs and cannot be solved, only simplified. They help represent real-world relationships.

Example: If a pencil costs $x, then the total cost for 4 pencils is 4x.

  • Like terms: Terms with the same variables and exponents (e.g., 3x and 7x).
  • Simplifying: Combine like terms (e.g., 3x + 2x = 5x).

Solving Simple Equations

Solving an equation means finding the value of the variable that makes the equation true.

Example: Solve 2x + 4 = 10

  • Step 1: Subtract 4 from both sides → 2x = 6
  • Step 2: Divide both sides by 2 → x = 3

Tip: Always do the same operation on both sides of the equation to keep it balanced.

Transposition Technique

Transposition means moving a term to the other side of the equation and changing its sign.

Example: x - 5 = 8 → x = 8 + 5 → x = 13

This technique is helpful when solving equations with multiple terms or variables.

Two-Step Equations

These are equations that require two operations to isolate the variable.

Example: 3x - 6 = 9

  • Step 1: Add 6 to both sides → 3x = 15
  • Step 2: Divide by 3 → x = 5

Common Algebra Formulas

  • Perimeter of Rectangle: P = 2(l + w)
  • Area of Triangle: A = ½ × b × h
  • Area of Circle: A = πr²
  • Speed Formula: Speed = Distance ÷ Time

Real-Life Applications of Algebra

  • Calculating budgets and savings
  • Determining travel time and distances
  • Measuring ingredients in cooking with variables
  • Predicting outcomes in data science and AI

Tips to Master Algebra

  • Always isolate the variable step by step
  • Use substitution to check your solutions
  • Practice simplifying and rearranging expressions
  • Work with real-life word problems to improve logic
