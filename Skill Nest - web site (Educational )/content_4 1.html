Mathematics

Master essential skills in arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and beyond

Lesson 1: Numbers & Operations

  • Types of Numbers: Natural, Whole, Integers, Rational, Irrational, Real
  • Even and Odd: Even = divisible by 2 (e.g., 2, 4, 6). Odd = not divisible by 2 (e.g., 1, 3, 5)
  • Prime Numbers: Numbers with exactly 2 factors (e.g., 2, 3, 5, 7, 11)
  • Composite Numbers: More than 2 factors (e.g., 4, 6, 8)
  • Factors & Multiples: Factors divide exactly. Multiples are repeated additions.
  • LCM & HCF: Least Common Multiple and Highest Common Factor are used in fraction operations.

Real-Life Uses: Dividing items equally, checking for common denominators in fractions.

Lesson 2: Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

  • Remember: P → E → MD → AS
  • Multiplication and Division: Left to right
  • Addition and Subtraction: Left to right
  • Nested Parentheses: Solve inner brackets first

Example: 4 + 6 × (2 + 1)² ÷ 3 = 16

TIP: Always write steps neatly to avoid errors.

Lesson 3: Understanding Fractions

  • Numerator: Number above the line (how many parts)
  • Denominator: Number below the line (total parts)
  • Common Denominator: Needed when adding or subtracting fractions
  • Reciprocals: Flip numerator and denominator (used in division)
  • Real-World: Baking, cooking, portioning, medicine dosage

Example: 3/4 + 1/4 = 4/4 = 1

Lesson 4: Decimals and Percentages

  • Decimal System: Based on powers of 10
  • Conversion: 0.5 = 50%, 3/4 = 0.75 = 75%
  • Applications: Bank interest, taxes, exam scores, discounts
  • Percent Increase/Decrease: Used in finance & price changes

Example: A shirt worth $200 with 20% off → Discount = $40, Final Price = $160

Lesson 5: Introduction to Algebra

  • Expression vs Equation: Expressions don't have "=" sign, equations do
  • Transposition: Moving terms across the equal sign while changing signs
  • Two-Step Equations: Solve in reverse order (undo operations)
  • Formulas: Perimeter = 2(l + w), Area = πr²

Example: 2x - 4 = 10 → 2x = 14 → x = 7

Lesson 6: Geometry Basics

  • Types of Triangles: Equilateral, Isosceles, Scalene
  • Circle Properties: Radius, Diameter, Circumference
  • Area Formulas: Triangle = ½ × base × height, Circle = πr²
  • Volume: Cube = s³, Cylinder = πr²h
  • Real-World: Blueprints, packaging design, architecture
