Lesson 4: Decimals and Percentages

Understand how decimals and percentages connect and apply in everyday life

Understanding Decimals

Decimals represent parts of a whole using place value after a decimal point. They are a more precise way of expressing numbers, especially when fractions are difficult to interpret or use.

  • Decimal Point: Separates whole numbers from fractional parts (e.g., 3.75 means 3 whole units and 75 hundredths).
  • Place Values:
    • 0.5 = 5 tenths
    • 0.25 = 2 tenths + 5 hundredths
    • 0.007 = 7 thousandths
  • Terminating vs Recurring Decimals: 0.5 (terminates), 0.333… (repeats infinitely)

Example: Add 3.5 + 1.25 → Align the decimal points → 3.5 = 3.50 → 3.50 + 1.25 = 4.75

Understanding Percentages

Percentages are a way to express numbers as parts of 100. It's commonly used in discounts, exam marks, population statistics, and more.

  • Definition: "Percent" means "per hundred". 75% means 75 out of 100.
  • Conversions:
    • Decimal to %: 0.8 = 80%
    • Fraction to %: 3/4 = 0.75 = 75%
    • % to Fraction: 60% = 60/100 = 3/5
  • Usage: Discounts, tax, success rates, interest rates.

Example: A jacket costs $120. A 25% discount means:

  • 25% of 120 = (25/100) × 120 = 30
  • Discounted Price = 120 - 30 = $90

Decimal and Percentage Relationship

Decimals and percentages are closely related. Every decimal can be converted into a percentage by multiplying by 100, and vice versa.

Decimal Percentage
0.25 25%
0.50 50%
0.90 90%

Real-Life Applications

  • Shopping: Understanding sale discounts and tax.
  • Banking: Calculating interest and savings percentages.
  • Health: Nutritional percentages on food labels.
  • Academics: Marks and grades are often shown in %.

Tips to Master Decimals and Percentages

  • Always align decimals properly when adding or subtracting.
  • Practice converting between decimals, fractions, and percentages.
  • Use 100 as a common base when comparing percentages.
  • Visualize percentages using pie charts or bar graphs.
